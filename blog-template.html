You Can't Track AI Like Traditional Search. Here's What to Do Instead.

By Louise Linehan • Reviewed by Ryan Law

Traditional search tracking is built on a simple promise: type a query, get a result, and track your ranking. AI doesn't work that way.

Assistants like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity don't show fixed results—they generate answers that vary with every run, every model, and every user.

"AI rank tracking" is a misnomer—you can't track AI like you do traditional search.

But that doesn't mean you shouldn't track it at all.

You just need to adjust the questions you're asking, and the way you measure your brand's visibility.

Why you can't track AI like traditional search

In SEO rank tracking, you can rely on stable, repeatable rules:

  • Deterministic results: The same query generally returns similar SERPs for everyone.
  • Fixed positions: You can measure exact ranks (#1, #5, #20).
  • Known volumes: You know how popular each keyword is, so you know what to prioritize.

AI breaks all three.

  • Probabilistic answers: The same prompt can return different brands, sources, and advice each time.
  • No fixed positions: Responses are fluid paragraphs, not ranked lists.
  • Unknown volumes: AI platforms rarely share query data.

Key insight: Traditional SEO tracking gives you precision. AI tracking gives you patterns. Both matter, but they answer different questions.

What to do instead

1. Aggregate AI answers to find out your actual visibility

Instead of tracking individual rankings, track how often your brand appears across multiple AI responses.

Run the same query 100 times across different AI models. If your brand appears in 30% of responses, that's your visibility score for that query.

2. See how your AI visibility ties to other channels

AI visibility doesn't exist in a vacuum. Track how it correlates with:

  • Organic search rankings
  • Brand search volume
  • Social media mentions
  • Direct traffic patterns

3. Track your AI Share of Voice in your market

Share of Voice (SOV) matters more than individual rankings. If there are 10 brands in your space and you appear in 40% of AI responses while competitors average 6%, you're winning—even without a "#1 ranking."

Figure 2: An AI visibility dashboard displaying share of voice analysis, competitor comparisons, and query performance metrics to track brand visibility across AI platforms.

How to calculate AI Share of Voice:

  1. Define your key queries (product searches, problem searches, comparison searches)
  2. Run each query multiple times across AI platforms
  3. Count brand mentions for you and competitors
  4. Calculate percentage share

4. Work out your relative ownership of topics

AI systems associate brands with topics. Understanding which topics you "own" in AI responses helps you identify strengths and gaps.

5. Learn a lot about your brand positioning

How AI describes your brand reveals how the internet collectively sees you. This is invaluable for understanding your actual market position versus your intended position.

6. Stay visible

The goal isn't to rank #1 (that doesn't exist in AI). The goal is consistent visibility across the queries that matter to your business.

Micro and macro AI tracking: You need both views

In a perfect world, tracking AI visibility on a micro and macro level isn't an either-or choice.

Micro tracking for high-stakes AI prompts

Micro tracking is about zooming in on the handful of queries that really matter to your business.

Figure 3: Keyword analysis dashboard highlighting high-value queries that matter most for business growth and should be prioritized in AI tracking efforts.

These might include:

  • Branded prompts: e.g., "What is [Brand] known for?"
  • Purchase-intent prompts: e.g., "Best [product category] for [use case]"
  • Comparison prompts: e.g., "[Your brand] vs [Competitor]"

Takeaway

That means being named in AI answers is no longer optional.

AI assistants are already part of the discovery journey.

People turn to ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot for product recommendations, not just quick facts.

If your brand isn't in those answers, you're invisible at the exact moment decisions are made.

That's why tracking AI visibility matters.

Even if the data is noisy, it shows whether you're part of the conversation—or whether competitors are taking the spotlight.

Final thoughts

No, you'll never track AI interactions in the same way you track traditional searches.

But that's not the point.

AI search tracking is a compass—it will show if you're headed in the right direction.

The real risk is ignoring your AI visibility while competitors build presence in the space.

Start now, treat the data as directional, and use it to shape your content, PR, and positioning.
